Emma Kopp
------
I am an PhD student in Statistics in the CEREMADE at Paris-Dauphine University with Christian Robert and Robin Ryder. My research focuses on Bayesian computational statistics (MCMC and ABC methods) and it’s applications to linguistics and food security. 
Last year I was part of the <a href="https://sites.google.com/view/ceremade-yrs" target="_blank">Young researchers of CEREMADE</a>. Feel free to send me a mail if you want to present your work!


I am currently working as a visiting student at La Sapienza in Rome with <a href="http://francescapanero.github.io/" target="_blank">Francesca Panero</a>, <a href="https://sahokoishida.github.io/"> Sahoko Ishida</a> and <a href="https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=wS4eqAsAAAAJ&hl=en">Rebecca Leygonie</a> on food insecurity in African countries, developing Bayesian Gaussian Process and Deep Learning models to optimize food aid distribution using WFP survey data.

Projects

------

- **Project 1**: **Time depth and the limits of phylogenetic inference in linguistics**. Through comprehensive simulations and infferance studies such as MCMC, we explore how time depth imposes limits on phylogenetic inference in historical linguistics. Building on theoretical results by <a href="https://www.stat.berkeley.edu/users/mossel/publications/jcb_impossibility.pdf">Mossel</a> on the impossibility of reconstructing ancestral data and phylogenies beyond certain temporal thresholds, we provide empirical bounds on reconstruction accuracy as a function of tree age. Our findings reveal that reconstruction accuracy deteriorates significantly beyond 9,000 years. We also demonstrate that increasing dataset size can improve reconstruction quality for ancient trees. This work provides quantitative insights into the temporal boundaries of reliable phylogenetic reconstruction in linguistics.

- **Project 2**: **Contrasting modes of cultural evolution: Kra-Dai languages and weaving technologies**. In this comparative study, we investigate the parallel evolution of languages and weaving technologies among Kra-Dai peoples across southern China and Southeast Asia using Bayesian phylogenetic methods. We uncover that while both languages and looms show nested phylogenies reflecting geographic migrations, they evolved through fundamentally different processes.

- **Project 3**: Spatio-temporal Gaussian process models and Deep Learning method to predict food insecurity at regional level. This is joint work with <a href="http://francescapanero.github.io/" target="_blank">Francesca Panero</a>, <a href="https://sahokoishida.github.io/">Sahoko Ishida</a> and <a href="https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=wS4eqAsAAAAJ&hl=en">Rebecca Leygonie</a>. We use data from the UN World Food Programme Hunger Monitoring Unit.
